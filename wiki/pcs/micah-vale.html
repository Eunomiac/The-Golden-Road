Parkour
Years of rooftop squatting left Micah with feral parkour instincts, so apartment blocks become ladders for hit-and-run ambushes.
Effect: Micah is a trained and proficient free runner. Free running is the art of moving fluidly through urban environments with complex leaps, bounds, running tricks, and vaults. This is the type of sport popularized in modern action films, where characters are unhindered by fences, walls, construction equipment, cars, or anything else the city puts in their way.
  • Flow (●): Micah reacts instinctively to any obstacles with leaps, jumps, and scaling techniques. When in a foot chase, subtract 5 from the successes needed to pursue or evade. Also, ignore up to -5 from environmental penalties to Athletics rolls.
  • Cat Leap (●●): Micah falls with outstanding grace. When using a Dexterity (+3) + Athletics (+4) roll to mitigate damage from falling, he gains one automatic success. Additionally, he adds 5 to the amount of damage removed through this roll. Parkour will not mitigate damage from a terminal-velocity fall.
  • Wall Run (●●●): When climbing, Micah can run upward for some distance before having to traditionally climb. Without rolling, Micah scales 30 feet as an instant action, rather than the normal 10 feet.
  • Expert Traceur (●●●●): Parkour has become second nature for Micah. By spending a Willpower point, he may designate one Athletics roll to run, jump, or climb as a rote action (reroll all failed dice once).
    Drawback: On any turn during which Micah uses this ability, he may not apply his Defense to oncoming attacks.
  • Freeflow (●●●●●): Micah's Parkour is now muscle memory. He can move without thinking, in a Zenlike state. Micah must successfully meditate Chronicles of Darknessp.75 in order to establish Freeflow. Once established, he is capable of taking Athletics actions reflexively once per turn. By spending a point of Willpower on an Athletics roll in a foot chase, he gains three successes instead of three dice.
